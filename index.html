Tower of Doom

by Arman Jordan and Marcus Williamson


Playable .zip download

Like the eye of Sauron in Lord of the Rings, you stand in VR atop a tall tower overlooking a vast landscape. Creatures march towards you from all directions, and you must beat them back
with spells that you cast using hand-tracking controls. In this vertical slice, when your hand is outstretched, a lightning spell can be cast to slice across the land and decimate your
enemies.

Tower of Doom is a PCVR game made in Unreal Engine 5. We created this vertical slice as our quarter-long senior capstone project for CMPM 171: Game Design Studio at UC Santa Cruz.
The goal of the project was to collaborate with a team to prototype and build a vertical slice that we could potentially expand upon and polish in the following course, CMPM 172.
As part of the assignment, our game was created with the following constraints, meant to make our games more accessible:

  • Playable with limited vision (e.g. colorblindness)
  • Playable with one hand
  • Playable without sound
  • Localized to support at least two languages
  • Under a certain filesize
  • Can be played in short bursts with natural breaks in gameplay
We had to be considerate of these requirements as we made choices about gameplay and how we conveyed information in our game. For example, the lightning spell playable in the
vertical slice can be cast with one hand, and just one hand at a time, and the enemies' health and numbers are balanced around one-handed gameplay. We also chose to convey
information visually or through gameplay rather than through text where we could, minimizing the amount of English reading necessary to play.

Team members

I came up with the idea, designed/programmed the game, created the environment, did the soundwork, the art choices/modeling, the spellcasting, visual polish, enemy/AI, pathfinding setup,
win and loss condition, and worked on VR support.

I implemented integration with VR and VR controls, as well as the splash screen. Getting hand tracking to work was a little tricky since this was my first major Unreal project
and support for hand tracking is relatively limited, but I found a workaround that would allow for hand tracking and controller to work.
